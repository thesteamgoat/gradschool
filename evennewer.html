Welcome to the Lesson

Too many people spend money unnecessarily on electricians because they do not possess basic skills and/or understanding about the flow of energy and electrical circuits. Knowing how to replace a light bulb when it burns out could save you from costly electrician visits and embarrassment. A simple issue such as this can easily be solved by an individual, but more serious electrical problems will require professional assistance. Having a basic understanding of how electricity flows through a complete circuit is an essential life skill to productive members of society who need to know how to proceed with any electrical problems in their dwellings (I can fix this myself vs. I need to call in an expert).

Therefore, as part of our energy unit, you will be required to construct a simple electrical circuit with three different loads: a light bulb, a buzzer, and a fan motor. Review this website for step-by-step instructions to help you. If you have constructed the circuit correctly, you should receive immediate feedback by activating the load. However, if you have not constructed the circuit correctly, you will need to troubleshoot to find the gap in your circuit.
